Terms of Use

QUESTIONS LAWSPOT DOESN'T ANSWER

LawSpot does not answer questions about conveyancing or property leasing (except residential tenancies), or questions from landlords, or from business ventures and employers. This is because LawSpot is aimed at addressing unmet legal need in the community.

LawSpot will only provide very basic information in relation to questions about wills, estates, property relationship agreements, powers of attorney and enduring powers of attorney. This is because LawSpot cannot assist with reviewing or drafting any documents in these areas due to the online Q&A format.

The Rules of Conduct and Client Care for Lawyers of the New Zealand Law Society require that we provide you in advance with the following initial information:

Professional indemnity insurance

The answers published on LawSpot are provided by different legal organisations or individual lawyers. Each LawSpot answer indicates which organisation or individual has provided the answer. Lawyers’ Fidelity Fund

The Law Society maintains the Lawyers’ Fidelity Fund for the purposes of providing clients of lawyers with protection against pecuniary loss arising from theft by lawyers. As the legal services we provide are free and we do not receive or hold money or valuables on trust, the fidelity fund is not relevant to the services offered by LawSpot.

COMPLAINTS PROCEDURE

Purposes

  • To provide a fair and timely process to resolve complaints about LawSpot.
  • To ensure that a quality service is provided to users of the Website.
  • To ensure that risks to LawSpot are identified and managed appropriately.
  • To ensure that all parties are protected by an explicit and clear procedure.

When will this procedure be used?

This procedure should be used for every complaint made about LawSpot, its operations, its partner law firms and lawyers, the CLWHV, or any of its volunteers.

Principles

The principles of natural justice apply to this procedure. This includes the right to be treated fairly and to state your side of events. Complaints will be taken seriously and the complainants will be treated with respect and courtesy. LawSpot will seek to resolve the complaint at the earliest stage and to learn from the complaint so that, where appropriate, action can be taken to ensure the situation does not happen again.

Making a complaint

  • Any person who has a complaint and who does not wish to resolve it informally can raise it with a member of LawSpot’s Management Team, by emailing complaint@lawspot.org.nz.
  • A complaint can be made through a representative. If the complainant wishes to remain anonymous, his or her request for anonymity will be considered by LawSpot in accordance with Part 4 of the Privacy Act 1993 – “Good reasons for refusing access to personal information”.

Investigating the complaint

  • LawSpot’s executive team will investigate the complaint in the first instance.
  • If the complaint is about a particular answer or particular advice provided on LawSpot, the complaint will be passed on to the publisher of that particular answer.
  • The name of the publisher will appear next to the published answer on the website. The publisher will be either CLWHV or one of LawSpot’s partner law firms or lawyers.
  • LawSpot will assist in the handling of complaints about CLWHV and LawSpot’s partner firms and lawyers. As such, references to “LawSpot” below include the CLWHV General Manager or Supervisor, and partners of LawSpot’s partner law firms or lawyers holding individual practicing certificates, as the context requires.
  • LawSpot will:
    • contact the complainant as soon as possible to acknowledge the complaint.
    • explain the complaints procedure to the complainant and, if the complainant wishes, will send to them a copy of the complaints procedure.
    • clarify the complainant’s concerns, discuss how the complainant believes the complaint can be resolved, and agree on timeframes.
    • contact the party or parties being complained about, put the complaint to them, and ask them for their interpretation of the situation and how they think the issue can be resolved.
    • conduct any other investigations as are necessary.
  • If the complaint is able to be resolved in a way that satisfies everyone involved, LawSpot will ensure that any required action is taken. LawSpot will then write to the complainant and to the other party or parties confirming the understanding that the complaint has been resolved.
  • Where a complaint concerning CLWHV is not resolved, the CLWHV complaints process will be available to the complainant. You can contact CLWHV to request a copy of their complaints policy.
  • Where a complaints concerning a LawSpot partner firm is not resolved, the complainant may refer his or her complaint to the New Zealand Law Society.
