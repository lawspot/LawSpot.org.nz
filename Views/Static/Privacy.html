Privacy Policy

Protecting your privacy matters to us. This document discloses our information gathering and usage practices in relation to the LawSpot website (the Website). For additional information about the Website please review our Terms of Use. We reserve the right to change our privacy policy from time to time but will notify our users about any such changes on the Website.

Use of your information

We collect personal information about you through your use of the Website, including your registration details and information relating to your use of the Website and the content you access.

We may use this personal information to help us provide LawSpot services to you, to verify your identity, to enforce or apply our terms and conditions, to protect the rights, property, or safety of LawSpot, our users, or others, for internal or government agency research purposes, for promoting other LawSpot services to you, and for any other use that you authorise.

We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties.

We release account and other personal information only when we believe release is appropriate for legal compliance and law enforcement (including to government agencies with statutory law enforcement responsibilities); to facilitate court proceedings; enforce or apply our terms and conditions; to develop community legal service delivery; or protect the rights, property, or safety of LawSpot, our users, or others. Users of the Website can contact us at support@lawspot.org.nz to ask whether such information has been provided.

User registration

Before you can submit a question on LawSpot, we require you to provide us with your email address and the region in which you live. We do not ask for your name. We require your email address so that we can notify you when your question has been answered. Your email address will never be published online or shared with our lawyer volunteers. We need to know which region you live in so that we can connect you to community legal services convenient to you. We may also use your email address to contact you and send you information about LawSpot and/or some of our affiliated partners.

Lawyer registration

You must be enrolled as a barrister and solicitor of the High Court of New Zealand and hold a current practising certificate in order to sign up to answer questions on LawSpot. As part of the lawyer registration process, we require you to provide us with your name, email address, the year in which you were admitted to the Bar, and the name of the law firm by which you are currently employed. You may also choose to specify the area of law in which you specialise (if any).

We require your email address so that we can notify you that your registration has been approved once LawSpot has verified that you hold a current practising certificate. Your name, firm name and year of admission is required to simplify this verification process. Your identity will never be disclosed to users of the Website. Your answer will be supervised by a Wellington Community Law Centre (WCLC) supervisor and published under the name of the WCLC.

We will also email you to notify you when there are unanswered questions open for you to answer. We may also use your email address to contact you and send you information about LawSpot and/or some of our affiliated partners.

Updating your account information

We provide you with ways in which you can update your personal account information, including changing your email address and password. We store previous email addresses against your profile to ensure compliance with our Terms and Conditions.

Email

If you are registered with LawSpot, we may send you emails about LawSpot, in addition to our customary question and answer-related communications. Further, it is our policy to remove any user from LawSpot’s mailing list upon that user’s request. We use email as a means of receiving feedback from the general public, and we encourage you to email us with your questions or comments.

We reserve the right to contact you via email to invite you to complete a feedback survey for us once you have had a question answered on LawSpot. The survey may ask you for personal and demographic information on a confidential basis, to be used to improve the LawSpot service. Completing the survey is voluntary. Survey responses will be processed by LawSpot administrative staff and not seen by any of LawSpot’s lawyer volunteers, to ensure that the user cannot be linked through the identifying information to the particular legal question they earlier posted. Data compiled through the survey may be shared by LawSpot with the Ministry of Justice for statistical and budgetary purposes.

Asking questions on LawSpot

Will my question be anonymous?
Yes, your question will be anonymous. When your question is submitted, your email is removed and LawSpot’s administrators will delete any part of your question that contains identifying features about you. Be aware that your question may be modified slightly before it is put into a pool of questions that our lawyer volunteers may choose to answer. At no point will a public user of the Website or a LawSpot lawyer volunteer have access to identifying details about you such as your email address. This is to ensure user anonymity and to prevent lawyer volunteers from being conflicted out of answering some users’ questions. Only LawSpot administrators will be able to view the question directly as submitted by you online. LawSpot may only use information that is personal to you for the limited purposes outlined above, in the “Use of your information” section of this policy.

Will my question be published online?
Yes, your question will be published online. When you submit a question on the Website, you will be asked to tick a box that asks if you agree to the Website’s Terms of Use. By ticking this box, you understand that your question and the answer will be published on the Website for public view. We make this content available as it may be helpful to other users. (But as the Important Notice appended to each answer makes clear, you should not rely on a generic answer provided on the Website even if you are in a similar situation, as small variations in fact can change a legal outcome or a lawyer’s conclusion.) Accordingly, you waive your right to claim confidentiality over the content of your question and the answer provided. No liability whatsoever is accepted by the authors or the publishers, for loss, damage or inconvenience consequent upon the question and answer being made public once you have indicated that you agree to share your answer with other users of the Website.

Answering questions on LawSpot

When you register to answer questions on LawSpot, you will be asked to tick a box that asks if you agree to LawSpot’s Policy for Lawyer Volunteers. By ticking this box, you understand that your answer will be published online under the name of the WCLC, and that supervisors from the WCLC reserve the right to modify or rewrite your answer before it is published online. Accordingly, you waive any rights to claim confidentiality over the content of the answer you submit. The Important Notice attached to every answer makes clear that no liability whatsoever is accepted by the authors or the publishers, for loss, damage or inconvenience consequent upon the question or answer being made public. Your identity will not be linked to the answer or shared with the public.

Control of your password

You are responsible for all actions taken with your LawSpot account, email address and password. Therefore, we do not recommend that you disclose your LawSpot password to any third parties. If you choose to share your user name, email address and password or your information with third parties to provide you additional services, you are responsible for all actions taken with this information and therefore you should review that third party's privacy policy.

It is important for you to protect against unauthorized access to your password and to your computer. Ensure you logout when you have finished visiting the Website, especially if you access the Website from a shared computer.

Cookies

Cookies are small pieces of information that are stored by your web browser in a file on your computer's hard drive when you use our Website. We use cookies to improve the experience of using our site. Cookies are widely used on most major websites.

We use "session" cookies to temporarily store information to help you use the Website. This information is used to deliver the right content to you as you use the Website and avoids unnecessarily asking you to log in. Session cookies expire immediately when you close your web browser.

We also use "persistent" cookies to personalise the user experience, to help protect our members and to provide us with research information (such as how often users visit LawSpot each month). Persistent cookies allow us to track users on LawSpot and help us identify users abusing the LawSpot service. A persistent auto-login cookie is also stored when LawSpot users select the "remember me" option when logging in. The auto-login cookie is removed when the LawSpot user logs out.

We reserve the right to participate in Google's AdWords pay-per-click advertising programme. AdWords advertisements appear on Google's search result pages and on the Google Network. Google can use cookies to measure the number of Google users that visit certain LawSpot web pages, or who complete certain actions on the Website. Google is able to provide this data to LawSpot without identifying the users. View Google's privacy policy.

Third party links

The LawSpot website may contain links to third party sites to enhance the user experience. We have no control of either the content or privacy policies of such sites, nor should such links be considered an endorsement of these sites except where otherwise noted. In some cases, third party advertisers may also use cookies in certain advertisements or links that appear on the Website for advertising purposes.

Other information we collect

We use additional information to do internal research on our users' demographics, interests, and behaviour to better understand and serve you and our community. This information may include the URL that you just came from (whether this URL is on our site or not), which URL you next go to (whether this URL is on our site or not), what browser you are using, and your IP address.

Users who login may have their IP addresses recorded. This information will only be provided to legal authorities in cases of suspected fraud or for law enforcement purposes. This information is stored securely and will not be provided to other members or non-authorised parties.

If you send us personal correspondence, such as emails, or if members of the LawSpot administrative staff send us correspondence about your activities on the Website, we may collect such information into a file specific to you.

Removal of registration

If you wish to have your registration removed from our directory for any reason, you may send us an email requesting this.

Updates to our information practices

From time to time, we make changes to our information practices or alter the functionality of our Website. We always update this statement accordingly, so we encourage you to periodically review this page for the latest information on our privacy practices.

Questions?

If you have any questions about our privacy policies or any other matter, please contact us at support@lawspot.org.nz.
